## 1) Clear Project Proposition

Jixia is a playable media design project that translates classical Chinese philosophical debate into strategic interaction.

This project is not a static cultural display.  
It tests how ideas become player decisions, and how decisions become interpretable cultural experience.

---

## 2) Aesthetic Position

The project follows three visual-interaction principles:

1. **Archive to arena**: literati-inspired atmosphere staged as a live debate court.
2. **Elegance under pressure**: calm interface rhythm with high-clarity tactical moments.
3. **Readable layers**: cultural mood first, decision readability always preserved.

---

## 3) Core Research Question

**How can philosophical thought be transformed from textual knowledge into actionable, perceivable, and discussable interaction?**

The design treats each match as a translation chain:
- philosophy -> rule grammar
- rule grammar -> player choice
- player choice -> cultural interpretation

---

## 4) Prototype Evidence (Vertical Slice)

The current prototype is deliberately constrained for review validity:

- **2 schools**: 礼心殿 and 玄匠盟
- **1 topic**: "Should virtue lead law, or should law lead virtue?"
- **1 battleground**: Jixia Standard Debate Court
- **12-20 cards**: current recommended set is 16
- **3 rounds**: full closed loop from setup to result

This is the proof unit: small enough to evaluate clearly, rich enough to demonstrate strategic and cultural intent.

---

## 5) Future Potential

After vertical-slice validation, expansion runs on three tracks:

1. **Design track**: scale to more schools and topics while preserving readability.
2. **Research track**: structured observation of how players interpret culture through play.
3. **Publication track**: portfolio film, exhibition-ready documentation, and academic-facing outputs.
