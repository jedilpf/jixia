# Research Proposal (English)

## Title
Translating Philosophy into Interaction: A Media Design Study Through a Debate-Based Vertical Slice

## Core Research Question
**How can classical philosophical thought be transformed into interaction that is playable, interpretable, and aesthetically coherent?**

## Why This Question Matters
Many culture-based projects stop at visual symbolism or narrative reference.  
This project tests a media design alternative: culture is treated as an interaction grammar, not an ornamental layer.

## Prototype as Research Instrument
The vertical slice is intentionally constrained to improve validity:
- 2 schools
- 1 topic
- 1 battleground
- 12-20 cards
- 3 full rounds

This narrow scope allows focused observation of whether philosophical identity truly changes player reasoning.

## Method
- **Session format**: guided prototype run + short reflection interview.
- **Observation focus**:
  - Decision readability (can players explain their choices?)
  - Cultural legibility (can players infer school identity through play?)
  - Aesthetic coherence (does the interface support meaning without losing clarity?)
- **Evidence forms**:
  - Round-by-round notes
  - Reviewer interpretation summaries
  - Comparative observations across repeated runs

## Expected Contribution
1. A concrete model for culture-as-interaction in game/media design.
2. A reusable vertical-slice method for portfolio and academic evaluation.
3. A bridge between design practice (prototype) and research framing (question-method-evidence).

## Future Expansion Path
1. Scale to more schools and topics while preserving readability.
2. Build a comparative study set across multiple prototype variants.
3. Produce exhibition-ready and publication-ready outputs from the same design pipeline.
