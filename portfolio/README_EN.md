# Jixia: Strategic Thought Record

## A Media Design Project (For KMD / Portfolio Review)

### 1) Clear Project Proposition
Jixia is a playable media system that translates classical Chinese philosophical debate into strategic interaction.

This is not a history-display project. It is a design experiment in turning ideas into decisions players can feel, compare, and reflect on.

### 2) Aesthetic Position
The visual and interaction language follows three principles:
- **Archive to arena**: literati-inspired textures and tones are staged as a live debate space.
- **Elegance under pressure**: calm visual rhythm with high-clarity decision moments.
- **Readable layers**: cultural atmosphere first, strategic readability always preserved.

### 3) Core Research Question
**How can philosophical thought be translated from textual knowledge into actionable, perceivable, and discussable interaction?**

The project treats each match as a media translation process:
- philosophy -> rule grammar
- rule grammar -> player choice
- player choice -> cultural interpretation

### 4) Prototype Evidence (Vertical Slice)
The current build is intentionally narrow to remain testable:
- **2 schools**: 礼心殿 and 玄匠盟
- **1 topic**: "Should virtue lead law, or should law lead virtue?"
- **1 battleground**: Jixia Standard Debate Court
- **12-20 cards**: current recommended set is **16**
- **3 full rounds**: complete loop from setup to outcome

This slice is the proof unit: small enough to evaluate, rich enough to demonstrate design intent.

### 5) Future Potential
After the vertical slice is validated, expansion follows three tracks:
1. **Design track**: additional schools and topics using the same readable debate grammar.
2. **Research track**: structured observation of how players interpret culture through play.
3. **Publication track**: portfolio film, exhibition-ready documentation, and academic-facing write-up.
